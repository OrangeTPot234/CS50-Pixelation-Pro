What is Pixelations?


Pixelations is a digital portfolio to display some of my work from this past semester and looks into one STEM student's foiree in art photography. The pixelations piece emphasizes how all the work is digital, and ultimately a discretization and some sense digitally abstracted form of reality—be that constructed or ‘real.’

What is Pixelations?


Pixelations started as part of an art class at Yale (ART 138 Digital Photography) that explores the creation of art with a camera. Through the photos you can see my struggle through and with composition, light, shadow, color, repetition, using a camera, and ultimately art, meaning, and beauty. Art making is a challenging process that pushes you to see and think about communicating an idea and translate that into a screen of pixels with color.
